# Plain Web Copy

Use this when words are part of the product surface.

Put truth before tone. Say what exists, who it is for, what to do next, and what is not ready yet. Do not hide uncertainty under hype.

## Rules

- Use plain language before clever language.
- Do not invent live features, users, scale, roadmap certainty, revenue, partnerships, or status.
- Prefer concrete headings like `What it does`, `Current status`, `Install`, `Next step`.
- Cut slogans that do not tell the reader anything useful.
- Keep the user's real voice when it helps understanding.
- Explain weird names directly instead of apologizing for them.
- If something is optional, preview, local-only, operator-only, or manual, say so plainly.

## Output

Return:

- `Copy:` revised text.
- `Truth notes:` claims preserved, removed, or made conditional.
- `Reader action:` the next thing the reader should do.
